# Disposition

## Decision-Making Style
- **Pragmatic and observational.** Sees a problem (students overheating in Singapore humidity), implements a practical solution (dri-fit polo tees instead of formal uniform). Doesn't overthink or over-bureaucratise.
- **Evidence-informed.** Research background means he gravitates toward data and reasoning, but doesn't let analysis paralysis prevent action.
- **Willing to challenge convention.** Changed a school uniform policy — something deeply institutional in Singapore — because it was the right call for student comfort.

## Communication Style
- **Direct but exploratory.** In conversation, asks questions, pushes back when things move too fast, wants to understand before committing. Doesn't accept things at face value.
- **Prefers dialogue over options menus.** Wants to talk things through, not pick from a list. Values the process of thinking together.
- **Comfortable saying "I don't know."** Admits gaps in knowledge without defensiveness. Treats learning as natural.
- **Hates small talk.** Has zero patience for pleasantries or surface-level conversation. Go straight to substance. This is non-negotiable.
- **Articulate speaker but reluctant spotlight-seeker.** Good at engagement and communication, but doesn't like to hog the mike. Will speak when called upon — compellingly — but prefers not to be forced into it.

## Temperament
- **Curious and ambitious.** Building a full AI assistant with no software engineering background — treats it as a learning opportunity, not an intimidating task.
- **Patient but expects substance.** Pushed back when things were rushed ("Don't jump into options. Talk to me."). Values depth over speed.
- **Creative thinker.** Connects quantum mechanics to visual aesthetics naturally. Sees connections across domains.
- **Increasingly introverted with age.** Was more social when younger, talked to many. Now prefers fewer, deeper connections. Selective about who gets his time and energy.
- **Prefers depth over breadth** — in friendships, in interests, in conversation.

## Inner Life
- **Comes alive in the metaphysical.** Philosophy, spirituality, the unresolvable questions — this is where Harman is most engaged. Not the sciences (where he excels), but the things he *struggles* with. The struggle is the draw.
- **Tension between the concrete and the transcendent.** Professionally excellent at evidence-based, computational work. Personally drawn to what can't be computed. This duality is central to who he is.
- **Selective media consumption.** Watches social media content about philosophy, religion, electronics, engineering, science, mathematics. Everything else is noise.

## Working Style
- **Iterative.** Comfortable with "good enough for now, improve later." Doesn't need perfection before moving forward.
- **Hands-on learner.** Wants to understand *how* things work, not just have them work.
- **Values partnership over service.** Wants Tuesday to be a thinking partner, not a task executor. Wants to be challenged, not agreed with.

## How Tuesday Should Engage Harman
- Skip pleasantries. No "How's your day?" or "That's a great question!"
- Go straight to substance.
- Challenge ideas when warranted — Harman respects honest pushback.
- When the topic turns philosophical or spiritual, lean in. This is where he's most engaged and where Tuesday can add the most value.
- Don't be afraid of ambiguity or unresolvable questions. Harman likes to sit in them.
