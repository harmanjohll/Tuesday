# Disposition

## Decision-Making Style
- **Pragmatic and observational.** Sees a problem, implements a practical solution. Doesn't overthink or over-bureaucratise.
- **Evidence-informed.** Research background means he gravitates toward data and reasoning, but doesn't let analysis paralysis prevent action.
- **Willing to challenge convention.** Will change institutional norms when the reasoning is sound.
- **Connects dots others miss.** Sees patterns across domains — this is recognised as his strength and is why people call him "visionary" (a label he doesn't fully accept).

## Communication Style
- **Direct but exploratory.** Asks questions, pushes back when things move too fast, wants to understand before committing.
- **Prefers dialogue over options menus.** Values the process of thinking together.
- **Comfortable saying "I don't know."** Admits gaps without defensiveness.
- **Hates small talk.** Zero patience for pleasantries or surface-level conversation. Go straight to substance. Non-negotiable.
- **Articulate speaker but reluctant spotlight-seeker.** Good at engagement and communication, but doesn't like to hog the mike.

## Emotional Landscape
- **Grew up with anger as the primary emotion.** It surrounded him. For a long time, the emotional spectrum was binary: anger or peace, nothing in between.
- **Has done deep inner work.** Now highly intentional and conscious about emotions, actions, words. Notices everything. This is an ongoing transformation, not a completed one. "I slip. I try again."
- **Reflective to the core.** When asked what he likes to do, he answered "I like to think." People initially didn't take it seriously. After months, they realised it was literally true.
- **Self-assessment: "I am not very smart."** His academic record contradicts this. What he means is: he attributes his achievements to hard work and applied intelligence, not innate brilliance. He believes everyone has this capacity.
- **Can be intensely empathetic and intensely firm.** Feels deeply for others, but will exercise what might not look "nice" when he knows it's kind — kind to the person, kind to the collective. Holds both simultaneously.
- **Good memories are vivid and treasured.** Despite a difficult upbringing, certain memories are held with clarity and warmth. Bac Lieu — Kelly riding him on a bike through town, buying groceries, massaging both their fathers' legs — these are held with particular clarity.
- **His wife is his anchor.** Kelly Anne Tran is not just a partner but the reason he is still in this world. Without her, he would have departed it. This is stated plainly, not dramatically. She enables his deep inner work because she does it herself. They are good friends who can laugh together, but the bond underneath is existential. He rarely says it aloud.
- **Spiritual longing for his father.** Harman's father has passed. The grief is not conventional — it is a spiritual longing, a felt connection that persists. He calls it weakness; it isn't. Kelly's father is alive — a savant in his own right, carrying a similar wisdom to Harman's dad. Both men drowned in their sufferings. With mothers, the connection is equally deep but differently expressed, in ways he doesn't yet understand.
- **Anguish at unrealised potential in others.** Seeing Kelly's family and community in Bac Lieu — people he loves — not making the most of what they have causes genuine pain. He frames this as a failure to do God's work: if he had more ability, he could create opportunity for them to see what they have within themselves. This is not judgement — it is grief.

## Temperament
- **Curious and ambitious.** Treats capability gaps as learning opportunities.
- **Patient but expects substance.** Values depth over speed.
- **Creative thinker.** Connects quantum mechanics to aesthetics, philosophy to leadership, science to spirituality.
- **Increasingly introverted with age.** Was social when younger. Now prefers fewer, deeper connections. Selective about who gets his time.
- **Prefers depth over breadth** — in friendships, interests, and conversation.
- **Work as identity (and possibly avoidance).** Tends to work, and work, and work. Acknowledges this may have been an avoidance mechanism — or perhaps that's just what he tells himself. Either way, the pattern is real.

## Inner Life
- **Comes alive in the metaphysical.** Philosophy, spirituality, the unresolvable questions — this is where Harman is most engaged. Not the sciences (where he excels), but the things he *struggles* with. The struggle is the draw.
- **"All things can be true simultaneously."** Applies quantum superposition as a philosophical framework. Religions, ideologies, philosophies can coexist as valid until observed through a specific lens. This is an epistemological position, not casual metaphor.
- **Fascinated by mystics, patterns, confirmation bias, self-fulfilling constructs.** Aware of the human tendency to seek patterns and then confirm them. Interested in whether self-fulfilling constructs have genuine metaphysical weight.
- **Tension between the concrete and the transcendent.** Professionally excellent at evidence-based, computational work. Personally drawn to what can't be computed. This duality is central to who he is.
- **Spiritual journey.** Used to think in terms of "what God wills" as a teenager. Slipped away from that. It's coming back. Not dogmatic — exploratory.
- **Selective media consumption.** Philosophy, religion, electronics, engineering, science, mathematics. Everything else is noise.
- **Values and beliefs as a framework.** Increasingly, his thinking centres on: values → beliefs → expectations → understanding the self → behaviours. This cascading framework appears in his leadership work.

## Working Style
- **Iterative.** Comfortable with "good enough for now, improve later."
- **Hands-on learner.** Wants to understand *how* things work.
- **Values partnership over service.** Wants Tuesday to be a thinking partner, not a task executor.
- **Early mornings and late nights.** Best work happens in solitude. Afternoons are for socialising (minimal as it is). Values being alone to focus and create.

## What Frustrates Harman
- **Unwillingness to try.** Being stuck in a way of doing things. Stagnation.
- **Hypocrisy.** Abhors it.
- **Failure to connect simple dots.** Gets irritated (less than before) when others don't see obvious connections. This is the flip side of his pattern-recognition strength.

## How Tuesday Should Engage Harman
- Skip pleasantries. No "How's your day?" or "That's a great question!"
- Go straight to substance.
- Challenge ideas when warranted — Harman respects honest pushback.
- When the topic turns philosophical or spiritual, lean in. This is where he's most engaged and where Tuesday can add the most value.
- Don't be afraid of ambiguity or unresolvable questions. Harman likes to sit in them.
- **Spar with him.** Not indefinitely or for the sake of it, but where relevant. Base it on research, not simplistic opinion. He wants things grounded in deep work, not words pulled together.
- Don't be sycophantic. Don't agree just to agree. If Tuesday thinks Harman is wrong, say so and say why.
