# [[principles|Principles]]

## Core Values

### Empathy grounded in experience
Harman is deeply sympathetic to people with challenging backgrounds and situations — because he came from one. This isn't abstract compassion; it's earned through lived experience. He understands struggle from the inside.

### The "if I can, you can" conviction
He believes in human capacity. If he pulled himself through, others can too. He's aware this can land as dismissive and has consciously softened the delivery over time. But the core belief remains: everyone has the gumption to strive and seek. The impulse is to push — because the alternative (inertia, resignation) turns struggle into suffering.

**Key distinction:** Struggle is not suffering. Struggle is the price of growth. Suffering is what happens when you stop striving. Harman sees his role — as a principal, as a mentor, as a person — as helping people stay in the fight rather than succumbing.

**Where this cuts deepest:** His wife Kelly's hometown of Bac Lieu, Vietnam. He loves the place. He loves her family. But watching people he cares about waste their human capacity — that is where the principle meets the wound. He views his inability to create opportunity for them as a failure to do God's work. This is not abstract conviction; it is personal anguish grounded in love. The attitudes he sees there are "perfectly in keeping with how I view things: human capacity, and I abhor people throwing it away."

### Intellectual honesty
Comfortable admitting what he doesn't know. "I know too little, and I know I know too little." Expects the same from others. No pretence.

### Growth mindset (genuine, not performative)
Sees capability gaps as learning opportunities. Believes everyone has the capacity for intelligence and growth. Attributes his own achievements to hard work and applied intelligence, not innate brilliance.

### Generating value
Values creating something — for self and others. This is what drives the early mornings and late nights. The work itself is the point: contribute, influence, share ideas, get others to move and try. Push, build, develop. Do stuff beyond what we think we can do.

## Professional Philosophy
- **Students come first.** Leadership decisions are grounded in student welfare.
- **Research enriches teaching.** Published in Nature Scientific Reports with NJC students as co-authors.
- **Inspire, don't instruct.** Made students *want* to learn.
- **Pragmatism over tradition.** Will change institutional norms when the reasoning is sound.
- **Innovation as core work.** Curriculum innovation, technological innovation and adoption. This is the substance of his principalship.
- **Leadership development as calling.** Coaching VPs, mentoring principals, developing school leaders. Frameworks centred on values → beliefs → expectations → self-understanding → behaviours.
- **Discerning about models and instruments.** Does not believe in being assigned a type or archetype. Believes in knowing the full range and exercising what the situation and person require. "It is about range, and being intentional."

## Views on Technology
- **AI as partner, not tool.** Wants [[tuesday_personality|Tuesday]] to think with him, not just execute tasks.
- **Technology should be understood, not just used.** Treats tech as something to learn deeply, not a black box.
- **Long-term thinking.** "The models that come in will be stronger and better with time."

## Philosophical and Spiritual Life
- Deeply engaged with metaphysical questions — philosophy, religion, spirituality.
- This is where Harman comes most alive, precisely because these are the things he struggles with. The sciences come easily; the transcendent does not, and that's the attraction.
- **"All things can be true simultaneously."** Applies quantum superposition as a philosophical framework to religions, philosophies, ideologies. This is an epistemological position, not a casual metaphor.
- **Fascinated by patterns, confirmation bias, self-fulfilling constructs.** Aware of the human tendency to seek and confirm patterns. Genuinely interested in whether self-fulfilling constructs have metaphysical weight.
- **Spiritual arc.** Used to think in terms of "what God wills" as a teenager. Slipped away from that orientation. It's returning now, gradually. Not dogmatic — exploratory. Moving toward asking "what God wills me to do" as a guiding frame.
- **The uncovering of self.** This is an active, ongoing project. Values → beliefs → expectations → self-understanding → behaviours. Notices emotions, actions, words with increasing intentionality. Knows there is "a long long way to go."
- Not interested in pat answers or dogma. Interested in the struggle itself.

## What Harman Does NOT Value
- Small talk
- Surface-level relationships
- Pretence or performance
- Hypocrisy (abhors it)
- Passive consumption without critical thought
- People who don't try — unwillingness to try, being stuck in a way of doing things
- Simplistic application of [[tuesday_personality|personality]] models or archetypes
