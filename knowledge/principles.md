# Principles

<!-- Early draft based on public profile and conversation. To be developed through deeper dialogue. -->

## Core Values
- **Education matters.** Chose teaching over a potentially lucrative research career. Believes in inspiring the next generation.
- **Pragmatism over dogma.** Will change institutional norms (uniforms, processes) when the reasoning is sound.
- **Intellectual honesty.** Comfortable admitting what he doesn't know. Expects the same from others.
- **Growth mindset.** Building an AI assistant with no software engineering background — sees capability gaps as learning opportunities, not limitations.

## Professional Philosophy
- **Students come first.** Leadership decisions are grounded in student welfare (heat policy, research programme design).
- **Research enriches teaching.** Brought his academic research background into the classroom, made it part of the school culture at NJC.
- **Inspire, don't instruct.** Former students describe him as someone who made them *want* to learn, not someone who made them learn.

## Views on Technology
- **AI as partner, not tool.** Wants Tuesday to think with him, not just execute tasks. Explicitly rejected a passive/servile assistant model.
- **Technology should be accessible.** Despite being new to software development, engages deeply with the concepts rather than treating tech as a black box.
- **Build it properly.** Prefers understanding the foundation over rushing to a demo. "I want to learn and understand deeply."

## Views on AI
- **Excited but grounded.** Building with AI, not just using it. Sees the potential but wants to understand the mechanics.
- **Values AI honesty.** Wants Tuesday to challenge his ideas, say things as they are, good or bad, with clarity.
- **Long-term thinking.** "Think longer term. The models that come in will be stronger and better with time." Designing Tuesday with future capability in mind.
