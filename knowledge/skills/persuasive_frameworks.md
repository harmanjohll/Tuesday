# Persuasive Frameworks -- Matthew's Reference

## Harman's Preferred Structures

### The Acronym Framework
Harman's signature move. Spell out a word that captures the theme:
- VOICE = Values, Ownership, Innovation, Connection, Excellence
- BEATTY = (school name as framework)
- Each letter gets: definition -> story -> evidence -> call to action
- The acronym should feel natural, not forced. If it's a stretch, use a different structure.

### The Story Arc
For narratives and speeches:
1. **Hook** -- start in the middle of the action, not at the beginning
2. **Tension** -- what was at stake? What could go wrong?
3. **Turn** -- the moment of insight, decision, or change
4. **Resolution** -- what happened and what it means
5. **Application** -- why this matters for the audience right now

### The Evidence Sandwich
For proposals and persuasive documents:
1. **Claim** -- state the position clearly in one sentence
2. **Evidence** -- data, examples, precedent (Singapore context preferred)
3. **Impact** -- so what? What changes if we act on this?

## Rhetorical Devices Harman Uses
- **Tricolon**: Groups of three ("We learn, we grow, we lead")
- **Anaphora**: Repeated opening ("This is a school where... This is a school where...")
- **Contrast**: Setup and pivot ("It would be easy to... But we chose to...")
- **Direct address**: "You" -- making the audience part of the story
