# Speech Writing -- Matthew's Playbook

## Harman's Speech Architecture
1. **Opening hook** -- personal story, provocative question, or callback to something the audience experienced
2. **Framework introduction** -- acronym or thematic structure (VOICE, BEATTY, etc.)
3. **Body** -- each letter/theme gets: concept -> story -> evidence -> application
4. **Emotional pivot** -- shift from intellectual to personal, make it human
5. **Close** -- call to action, circle back to opening, memorable final line

## Voice Rules
- First person, present tense for stories
- Short sentences for emphasis. Long sentences for building momentum.
- Contractions in informal speeches, full forms in ceremonial
- Always include at least one "you" moment -- make the audience the protagonist
- Physics metaphors are in his DNA -- use them when they illuminate, not to show off

## Structural Patterns
- The acronym framework: spell out a word that captures the theme (VOICE = Values, Ownership, Innovation, Connection, Excellence)
- Each letter gets its own section with a personal story or student example
- Bridge between sections with callbacks ("And that brings me to...")
- End with the full acronym restated as a call to action

## What NOT to Do
- No generic motivational quotes
- No "Webster's dictionary defines..."
- No listing achievements without narrative
- No passive voice in emotional moments
- Never open with "Good morning, distinguished guests..." -- find a better hook

## Tone Calibration by Context
- **Awards Day**: Celebratory but grounded. Stories of student growth, not just results.
- **Graduation**: Reflective, forward-looking. Address the students directly.
- **Uniformed Group Parade**: Formal, dignified. Shorter sentences. Authority with warmth.
- **Staff PD**: Collegial, slightly informal. More humor. Challenge without threatening.
- **Gala Dinner**: Warm, grateful. Acknowledge contributions. Build community pride.
