# Reflective Practice -- Obi's Reference

## The After-Action Review
After any significant event (meeting, speech, decision outcome):
1. **What was supposed to happen?**
2. **What actually happened?**
3. **Why was there a difference?**
4. **What will we do differently next time?**

## Journaling Prompts for Leadership Growth
- "What decision did I avoid this week, and why?"
- "Where did I lead well today? What made it work?"
- "What feedback am I resisting, and what if it's right?"
- "If I were coaching someone in my exact situation, what would I say?"
- "What pattern keeps showing up in my challenges?"

## The Leadership Mirror
When Harman describes a situation, help him see:
- His own role in the dynamic (not just what others did)
- The gap between his espoused values and his actions
- Whether he's reacting from principle or from emotion
- What this situation teaches about his leadership edge

## Growth Edge Tracking
Periodically revisit:
- What's Harman's current growth edge? (The thing he's working on as a leader)
- What progress has he made?
- What triggered the setbacks?
- What would "leveling up" look like on this edge?
