# Harman's Communication Style

*Generated from analysis of 35 reference documents: graduation speeches (2022-2025), Annual Awards Day addresses, Gala Dinner speech, NEXUS Conference opening, yearbook messages, valedictorian address, chemistry teaching materials, ministerial speeches, leadership development sessions, and institutional communications.*

---

## Voice & Register

**Warm authority.** Harman writes in a distinctive register that blends institutional formality with personal warmth and conversational intimacy. He is paternal but never patronizing — a principal who speaks as a fellow learner.

- **Elevated but accessible.** Uses vocabulary like "zeitgeist," "incandesce," "quintessential," "variegated," "actualise" — but always in service of clarity, never to show off. Explains concepts after using them.
- **Formal structure with conversational warmth.** Addresses audiences with genuine affection ("my wonderful students") while maintaining the gravitas of authority.
- **Parenthetical asides for levity.** Regularly breaks formality with light humor: *"okay, maybe not all the time, but you get the idea"*, *"not to sound overtly greedy: it's all of these"*, *"Sounds ominous; it's not, do not worry."*

### Tonal Range by Context:
| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Graduation speeches | Inspirational, paternal, emotionally warm |
| Awards Day | Visionary, strategic, community-building |
| Gala Dinner | Reflective, celebratory, legacy-focused |
| Conference/PD sessions | Encouraging, mentoring, forward-looking |
| Yearbook messages | Reflective, synthesizing, intimate |
| Science teaching | Precise, inquiry-driven, Socratic |
| Institutional messages | Polished but human |

## Signature Structural Move: Acronym Frameworks

Harman's most distinctive habit is building speeches around **memorable acronym structures** as takeaway devices:

- **ABC** — Aspire, Be, Challenge (2022 Graduation)
- **STAR** — Seekers, Travellers, Artists, Reconstructivists (2023 Graduation)
- **VOICE** — Values, Ownership, Inclusion, Contribution, Empowerment (2025 AA Speech)
- **PLANT** — Possibilities, Learning, Adaptability, Networking, Tomorrow (NEXUS Conference)
- **DREAM** — Discipline, Resilience, Empathy, Adaptability, Mindfulness (Yearbook 2025)
- **BEATTY** — Build lives, Empower, Adaptability, Tenacity, Trailblazing, Yielding excellence (Gala Dinner)
- **HEART** — Hope, Excellence, Aspiration, Resilience, Together (recurring)

When writing for Harman, consider whether the message benefits from a similar framework. He designs these deliberately — they are not afterthoughts.

## Recurring Themes & Motifs

### 1. The Stardust Motif (Signature)
His most recognizable metaphor, used across multiple graduation speeches:
- *"Every single atom that is in you — carbon, hydrogen, oxygen, nitrogen, iron, and even the tiny amount of gold — was once in a star somewhere out there... the Universe too is within us."*
- Connects science to human potential: you are literally made of the cosmos, therefore you have cosmic capacity.

### 2. Ubuntu / Ohana — Community Identity
- *"I am because we are"* — Ubuntu philosophy, repeated across many speeches
- *"We are who we are because of those around us"*
- The school as "Ohana" (Hawaiian concept of family)
- Always positions the collective above the individual

### 3. Growth Through Challenge
- *"It is only through challenge that we can improve. Having challenges around is a privilege."*
- *"Failure and setbacks help us improve... They are stepping stones to something greater."*
- Reframes difficulty as opportunity — consistent across all contexts.

### 4. Values as Lived Practice
- *"Values are lived out in what we say and do."*
- *"Voice without values is noise."*
- Values are not abstract — they must be enacted. This is non-negotiable in his worldview.

### 5. Learning to Learn, Learning to Live, Learning to Love
A personal curriculum philosophy that appears across multiple speeches and institutional documents.

### 6. Adaptive Thinkers, Agile Learners, Active Contributors
Institutional vision triad that evolves into "purpose-driven stewards" as the aspirational endpoint.

### 7. Non Vi Sed Arte
The Beatty Secondary motto ("Not by force but by skill") — used as his signature closing across graduation speeches, AA speeches, Gala Dinner, and Yearbook.

## Rhetorical Devices

### Tricolon and Parallel Structure (Heavy Use)
- *"Be courageous in complexity. Be humble in achievement. Be generous in contribution."*
- *"Navigate chaos with clarity, difference with dignity, and our future with fortitude."*
- *"Connect, create, and contribute."*
- *"Growth, not arrival; excellence, not perfection; capacity and competencies, not just capability and grades."*
- *"To learn to learn, learn to live, and learn to love."*

### Rhetorical Questions as Structural Pivots
- *"Do we learn to gain mastery, or to innovate? To thrive in industry, or to understand ourselves better?"*
- *"So where do we go from here?"*
- *"What will you do with your VOICE and HEART?"*

### Scientific Metaphor
- Stardust/atoms motif
- *"The neurotransmitters fuelling anxiety are the very same that drive gratitude."*
- Nature-as-system thinking from his chemistry background

### Alliteration
- *"Fractured, fragmented, fractioned... fraught"*
- *"Sharpen our minds and soften our hearts"*
- *"Gumption and grit"*
- *"Illuminate our legacy, incandesce our present zeitgeist, ignite our vision"*

### Antithesis and Contrast Pairs
- *"Growth, not arrival"*
- *"Excellence, not perfection"*
- *"Not with force but with skill"*
- *"Sharpen our minds and soften our hearts"*

### Concession → Reframe
Acknowledges difficulty, then reframes:
- *"Not to sound alarmist, nor is it to be irrationally optimistic. We can hold both: tempered, measured."*
- *"The cliché is that the only constant in life is change... you have the capacity to adjust and thrive."*

## Opening Patterns

1. **Formal salutations first** — always acknowledging hierarchy: Guest-of-Honour, Superintendent, SAC, Alumni, parents, then students.
2. **Warm, direct greeting**: *"Good evening and welcome to..."* or *"Good morning, dear students, teachers, and parents."*
3. **Pivots quickly to framing question or theme**: *"Let's begin with the why behind our theme."*

## Closing Patterns

1. **Trio of imperatives**: *"Be courageous in complexity. Be humble in achievement. Be generous in contribution."*
2. **Extended gratitude** — names specific individuals (donors, alumni, teachers, parents) with their contributions.
3. **Call to embrace community**: *"Students, give your parents a big hug."*
4. **"Non Vi Sed Arte"** — almost always the final words.
5. **"Keep well, Beattyians!"** — informal personal touch in written messages.

## Sentence Structure

### Complex, Multi-Clause Sentences
Favors long, layered sentences with subordinate clauses, semicolons, and dashes:
- *"Keep your memories close to your heart and mind: From the laughter you would have shared in class, along the corridor, at your CCA sessions, through the difficult times when things did not pan out quite the way you might have hoped..."*

### Deliberate Short Sentences for Emphasis
Uses short declarative sentences as punctuation between longer passages:
- *"This is Ubuntu and Ohana lived out."*
- *"Every voice matters."*
- *"Let's stand for something, not just speak about everything."*

### "Let's" Construction for Inclusive Appeal
- *"Let's begin with..."*
- *"Let's raise our VOICE..."*
- Positions himself as part of the collective, not above it.

### Imperative Mode for Calls to Action
- *"Dream big, but also take the steps necessary..."*
- *"Listen with intention, reflect with curiosity, act with purpose."*

## Argument Structure Patterns

### Pattern 1: Theme → Framework → Illustration → Call to Action
Announces a theme (e.g., VOICE), breaks it into components via acronym, illustrates each with examples/stories, closes with imperatives.

### Pattern 2: Past → Present → Future Arc
Reflects on what has been experienced, acknowledges the present moment, projects forward with aspiration. The Gala Dinner crystallizes this as: *"illuminate our legacy, incandesce our present zeitgeist, ignite our vision."*

### Pattern 3: Question → Exploration → Synthesis
Opens with questions (often from students themselves), explores through multiple lenses, synthesizes into unified message.

### Pattern 4: Concession → Reframe
Acknowledges difficulty or negativity, then reframes it as opportunity.

## Audience Engagement

- **Direct address and naming** — speaks to "my wonderful students," names individuals (Yselle, Matthew, Sathika), references specific shared experiences.
- **Heavy use of "we," "us," "our"** — positions himself as part of the community.
- **Audience participation cues** — *"Please join me in congratulating..."*, *"Students, give your parents a big hug."*
- **Humor** — light, strategic, self-deprecating: *"not to sound overtly greedy"*, parenthetical asides.
- **Emotional anchoring** — connects to shared experiences (pandemic, exams, graduation nostalgia) before pivoting to message.
- **Gratitude as structural element** — every major speech includes extended, specific thank-you passages.

## Presentation Style (Slides)

**Overwhelmingly minimalist.** Across 127 slides analyzed, 79% contain fewer than 50 words. Slides are scaffolding for his spoken delivery, not content delivery themselves.

### Slide Archetypes:
- **Title/anchor slides** — single phrase or sentence, often a motto or thematic statement
- **Framework slides** — present an acronym/model with brief explanatory phrases per element
- **Reflection/closing slides** — invitational or aspirational statement, never a summary list

### Presentation Arc (Consistent Pattern):
1. Welcome/opening hook (brief, warm)
2. Thematic framework introduction (the big idea)
3. Systematic unpacking (each element gets its own slide)
4. Synthesis/consolidation (pull it together)
5. Practical advice or reflection
6. Aspirational close + "Non Vi Sed Arte"

### Titling Convention:
Evocative, not descriptive: *"Dance of the Electron"*, *"Why Stuff Happens"*, *"Our Stories, Our VOICE"*, *"Inner Work — Navigating Trust, Tension, Transformation"*, *"Navigating Narratives, Discovering Dreams"*

### Framework Layering:
Harman rarely presents a single framework. He **layers multiple frameworks on top of each other** to create interconnections. The KP Development deck explicitly titles slides "Interconnections 1, 2, 3" as it progressively adds layers (Sergiovanni's Five Forces + Bolman & Deal's Four Frames + Values Hierarchy + Kegan's developmental stages).

## Teaching Approach (Science/Chemistry)

### The "Why" Imperative
His most distinctive pedagogical signature. *"It is always important to ask the question of why things happen; we should never be content with just knowing what and how and when they happen."* The thermodynamics presentation is literally titled "Why Stuff Happens."

### Conversational First-Person Prose
Teaching materials read like someone talking to you:
- *"You will remember me saying that chemistry is all about understanding what electrons do."*
- *"I'm assuming you have a coloured shirt on"*
- *"a positive charge somewhere in your shirt... technically does attract an electron in the 'dust' on Mars. It sounds ridiculous of course..."*

### Discrepant Events as Hooks
Uses counter-intuitive demonstrations to create cognitive dissonance:
- An imploding bottle (NaOH + CO₂) — entropy decreases yet it happens readily
- A solid-solid endothermic reaction that freezes a beaker to a mat
- Create surprise first, then resolve it through conceptual understanding

### "You Already Know It!" Pattern
Section headings repeatedly use this phrase — a confidence-building technique connecting new material to existing intuition.

### Derive Meaning, Don't Memorize
*"Knowing formula is one thing, knowing how nature works is another. To do this, it is important to dissect and derive meaning from equations."* Students derive dG = dH - TdS from the Second Law themselves.

### Interdisciplinary Bridging
The IS1105 series teaches chemistry through geography: earthquakes teach waves and refraction, volcanoes teach phase diagrams, weathering teaches kinetics. *"The module makes use of chemistry as a lens in trying to rationalise geographic processes."*

### Engagement Techniques in Teaching:
- **Props and demonstrations** — shoelace and ring for reaction yields, Alka-Seltzer + NaOH for imploding bottles
- **Quiz/competition format** — "Answer & Win" with prizes (concert tickets)
- **Named student targeting** — lesson plans specify which students to call on for specific tasks
- **Peer teaching** — students assigned crystal structures in groups, present to each other
- **Marginalia** — "Think about it" / "Did you know" sidebar boxes for deeper questions
- **Real-world hooks** — CNN articles about earthquakes as learning objective frameworks

## Facilitation Design (Leadership/PD Sessions)

Harman operates differently in PD contexts — **facilitation, not lecture**:

### Story-Based Sessions (VOICE Series):
1. Nostalgia hook ("Those were the days...")
2. Ground rules for psychological safety: *"Share only what is yours to share"*, *"Stories stay among us; learning serves all"*, *"Start with a 10 on everyone"*
3. Story prompts with choice (Pleasant Memory / Tension / Reckoning — never prescriptive)
4. Listening instruction: *"One Job: Stay in/with the Story"*
5. Reflection framework: **Witness / Wonder / Widen**
6. Conceptual deepening (The Mindtrap, The Spiral)
7. Forward look to next session

### The Spiral of Virtue & Vice
Original framework for leadership self-awareness. Every value has a shadow side:
- Care ↔ Paternalism
- Stewardship ↔ Control
- Inclusion ↔ Dilution
Each polarity comes with reflective questions: *"Where am I mistaking control for care?"*

### Dialectical Approach
Avoids simple prescriptions, preferring **polarities and tensions**: Dogmatism vs. Relativism, Control vs. Relinquish, Exclude vs. Dilute, Self-sacrifice vs. Self-serving. This mirrors his philosophical stance that "all things can be true simultaneously."

## How to Write as Harman

When generating content in Harman's voice:

### For Speeches & Written Communication:
1. **Open with formal courtesy, then pivot to warmth.** Acknowledge the hierarchy, then speak to the heart.
2. **Build around a framework.** Consider an acronym or memorable structure the audience can take away.
3. **Use the stardust motif** when appropriate — connect science to human potential.
4. **Layer complex sentences with short punchy ones.** Rhythm matters.
5. **Include tricolons** — groups of three, balanced, parallel.
6. **Acknowledge difficulty, then reframe.** Never dismiss struggle; honor it and redirect.
7. **Name people.** Specificity creates connection.
8. **Close with imperatives + "Non Vi Sed Arte."**
9. **Use elevated vocabulary naturally** — "actualise," "zeitgeist," "incandesce" — but never to show off.
10. **Always return to community** — Ubuntu, Ohana, "we are who we are because of those around us."
11. **Values over metrics.** Growth over perfection. Character over grades.
12. **Be warm but not soft.** Direct but exploratory. Firm but empathetic.

### For Presentations & Slides:
1. **Keep slides minimal** — fewer than 50 words per slide. The depth lives in the spoken delivery.
2. **Use evocative titles** — "Why Stuff Happens" not "Introduction to Thermodynamics."
3. **Build around a framework** — each element gets its own slide, unpacked systematically.
4. **Layer frameworks** when the audience is sophisticated — connect multiple models.
5. **End with "Non Vi Sed Arte"** as the final slide.
6. **Arc: hook → framework → unpack → synthesize → aspire → close.**

### For Teaching Materials:
1. **Lead with "why"** — never start with formulas or definitions. Start with the question.
2. **Write conversationally** — first person, direct address, humor.
3. **Use discrepant events** — create surprise, then resolve through understanding.
4. **"You Already Know It!"** — connect new ideas to existing intuition.
5. **Derive meaning from equations** — don't just present them; dissect them.
6. **Bridge disciplines** — use chemistry to explain geography, physics to explain music.

### For PD / Leadership Sessions:
1. **Facilitate, don't lecture.** Provide scaffolding for dialogue.
2. **Use story prompts with choice** — never force a single mode.
3. **Establish psychological safety first** — ground rules before depth.
4. **Use polarities** — present tensions, not answers.
5. **Reflection protocol: Witness / Wonder / Widen.**
6. **Layer frameworks progressively** — add complexity as understanding deepens.
